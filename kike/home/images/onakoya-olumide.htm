ONAKOYA Olumide.

Olu Onakoya was born on 21 October, 1951 in Lagos where he had his Primary School Education.

He was admitted into Government College Ibadan (GCI) in January, 1965. He was in Field House. He had a balanced life at GCI: , active in Sports (Cricket, Soccer and Athletics), livey in Academics (Best School Certificate Result in 1969), and also in extra-curricular activities (President, Literary and Debating Society in 1971; Editor, College Newspaper - "The Beacon" in 1971 and Winner, J.F. Kennedy Essay Competition in Western Region in 1971). He had early exposure to leadership as Head of Field House and School Prefect in 1971. He passed his Higher School Certificate Examination in 1971.

In September 1972, he won a Federal Government of Nigeria Scholarship to study Chemical Engineering at the University of Leeds, England and qualified in 1976 after which he returned to Nigeria.

Olu joined Mobil Oil Nigeria in 1977 as an Engineering Trainee. He had a varied career involving assignments in Terminal Operations, Lubricants, Plastic Container and Insecticide Manufacturing, Corporate Planning, Project Management, Facilities Management, Human Resources, Marketing and Business Leadership.

In 1992, he became the first Nigerian to win the Mobil Oil Nigeria Chairman's Award for Engineering Excellence due to his stewardship of the world class Mobil Head Office and Residential Projects in Lekki, Victoria Island, Lagos. He went on to win a Global Quality Award from Mobil Corporation in 1993 for his engineering work and business leadership of the same facilities.

In October 1997, after an outstanding performance in assignments with Mobil Corporation at Corporate Headquarters in Fairfax, Virginia and Los Angeles, USA, he was appointed Marketing Director, Mobil Oil Nigeria.

In January 2000, he was appointed General Manager, Mobil Oil East Africa, Addis Ababa, Ethiopia from where he moved to Accra in December 2001 as Chairman/Managing Director, Mobil Oil Ghana.

In August 2003, Olu moved to Harare as the Chairman/Managing Director of Mobil Oil Zimbabwe.

On April 1 2004, Olu Onakoya became the Chairman/Managing Director of Mobil Oil Nigeria. By this appointment, he became the first Nigerian to head the multinational company in 100 years of operations in Nigeria. He retired voluntarily in October 2008 after 31 years of service.

Olu has demonstrated total commitment to GCI and GCIOBA at Class Set, Lagos Branch and National levels. He ascribes his career and personal achievements to a solid foundation laid in the best traditions and pedigree of GCI... all round excellence, fairpaly, team spirit, honesty, integrity and responsible leadership and has no reservations whatsoever about giving his best back to the School.

He has been recognised by his Class Set (1965 Class Set) three times for excellence (1993, 1996 and 2004), and won the GCIOBA Lagos Branch Merit Award in 2007. He has supported the employment of several young Old Boys and the mentoring of the children of many ancient Old Boys. He has also part-sponsored the activities of the National GCIOBA, Lagos Branch GCIOBA and his Class Set on many occasions. He was the Chairman of the GCIOBA Ibadan Branch 2014 Annual Reunion tagged "A Day of Reminiscences".

Olu is married to Dr (Mrs) Aramide Onakoya, a Consultant Chemical Pathologist and they have three children.

He is enjoying an active life in retirement, reading, playing chess, mentoring young people on business and life skills, and sharing his knowledge and experience within the Oil Industry and the Lagos Business School.

For these and many other outstanding achievements, Olumide Abiola Onakoya is strongly recommended for the 2015 GCIOBA National Merit Award.
